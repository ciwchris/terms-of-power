# Terms of Power  
## Project Overview

## 1. Purpose

**Terms of Power** exists to make visible and contest the imbalance embedded in modern digital terms of service.

Most consumer-facing digital products rely on policies that allow extensive data collection, sharing, and reuse. These terms are often:
- difficult to understand
- non-negotiable
- disconnected from how companies present themselves publicly

The project treats these terms not as legal formalities, but as **instruments of power** that shape the relationship between users and organizations.

---

## 2. Core Problem

Modern data practices reflect a form of **data extraction** where:

- User behavior is treated as raw material
- Consent is broad, implicit, or forced
- Enforcement of privacy protections is inconsistent or ineffective

At the same time:
- Individual users have little leverage
- Large platforms are resistant to change
- Mid-sized companies rely on standardized legal templates they do not fully control

This creates a gap between:
- **brand trust**
- and **actual contractual obligations imposed on users**

---

## 3. Strategic Insight

Rather than targeting dominant technology platforms directly, this project focuses on:

### Mid-sized, consumer-facing organizations

These organizations:
- depend heavily on customer trust
- are sensitive to reputational risk
- often inherit problematic terms from third-party systems (ads, analytics, AI tools)

This makes them uniquely vulnerable to **public accountability around their terms**.

---

## 4. Objective

The objective of Terms of Power is to:

> **Create measurable pressure on organizations to revise harmful or excessive data terms by making those terms visible, specific, and reputationally costly.**

This is not a legal campaign or a boycott movement.

It is a **coordinated pressure strategy** based on:
- clarity
- visibility
- and collective action

---

## 5. Method

The project operates through structured public campaigns.

### Step 1: Identify Target Organizations
- Mid-sized brands with strong customer-facing identities
- Clear mismatch between brand messaging and data practices

---

### Step 2: Audit Terms

Focus on specific, concrete clauses such as:
- geolocation sharing
- third-party data access
- model training consent
- indefinite data retention

Avoid general complaints. Only highlight:
- language that is explicit
- and defensible as problematic

---

### Step 3: Coordinated Writing

Participants send structured messages to the organization:

**Characteristics:**
- written as real customers
- specific references to terms
- explicit tradeoffs ("this affects whether I continue using your service")

---

### Step 4: Public Visibility Layer

Private outreach is paired with controlled public exposure:
- professional networks
- public forums
- review platforms

The goal is not outrage, but **visible contradiction**:

> “This is what your brand says. This is what your terms enforce.”

---

### Step 5: Iteration and Escalation

If organizations respond:
- highlight improvements
- reinforce positive change

If they do not:
- increase visibility
- expand participation

---

## 6. Position on Infrastructure

Terms of Power operates within existing internet infrastructure.

- It does not attempt to withdraw from or boycott platforms
- It does not require ideological alignment from service providers

Instead, it applies pressure **from within the system**

This reflects the core belief:

> Change is most effective when contradictions are made visible where they already exist.

---

## 7. Expected Outcomes

**Short-term:**
- Increased awareness of specific terms among users
- Direct responses from targeted organizations

**Medium-term:**
- Revisions to publicly exposed clauses
- Internal review of third-party dependencies (ads, analytics, AI tools)

**Long-term:**
- Shift in how organizations treat terms as part of brand trust
- Greater alignment between user expectations and contractual reality

---

## 8. What This Is Not

- Not a general privacy awareness campaign  
- Not a legal reform initiative  
- Not a boycott or exit movement  
- Not targeted at individuals or employees  

---

## 9. What Success Looks Like

- A company removes or narrows a clause after public scrutiny  
- Terms become a visible part of brand accountability  
- Users recognize that coordinated action can influence policy  

---

## 10. Working Name

**Terms of Power (TOP)**

This name reflects the central idea:

> Terms are not neutral. They are expressions of power in the user–company relationship.
